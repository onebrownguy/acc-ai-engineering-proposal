🚀 Leveraging ACC's Existing AI Infrastructure

Activating Institutional Assets for Student Success

Strategic Analysis | October 13, 2025

📊 Executive Summary

Austin Community College already has significant AI infrastructure in place — institutional memberships, dedicated facilities, and proven partnerships. The strategic opportunity is not to build from scratch, but to activate existing assets for systematic student access.

🏛️ Infrastructure Already Built ACC has CGCS (Center for Government & Civic Service) facility at Rio Grande Campus + Full Membership in Austin AI Alliance (120+ member consortium) + proven track record with Scale AI Hackathon.
⚠️ Critical Gap Identified Infrastructure serves institutional needs (governance, policy, faculty development) but lacks systematic student activation — students need structured pathways to access Alliance resources, mentorships, and industry partnerships.
💡 Strategic Opportunity Reframe proposal from "ACC needs to build AI programs" to "ACC needs to activate existing infrastructure for students" — leverages institutional buy-in, existing partnerships, and proven facilities.

🎯 Activation Strategies: From Infrastructure to Student Impact

1
Leverage CGCS as AI Program Hub

CGCS facility already exists at Rio Grande Campus with event space, labs, and civic partnership model. Expand mission from civic leadership to AI + civic innovation.

  • Physical Space Activation: Designate CGCS Building 3000 as home base for AI student programs — tool access labs, mentorship sessions, industry office hours
  • Program Integration: Partner CGCS Director Bryan Port with CSIT Division (Manuel Duran) to co-manage AI technical programs alongside civic programs
  • Facility Scheduling: Reserve CGCS space for weekly AI student workshops, industry mentorship sessions, portfolio review events
  • Equipment Hosting: If ACC acquires VR/AR equipment or GPU workstations (see competitive analysis), house at CGCS for equitable multi-campus access

2
Activate Austin AI Alliance Student Pathways

ACC pays for Full Membership ($X,XXX/year institutional fee). Maximize ROI by creating structured student participation pathways.

  • Student Working Group Seats: Negotiate with Alliance for dedicated student representative seats in working groups (research, education, policy)
  • Mentorship Match Program: Partner with Alliance's 120+ member companies for structured 1:1 industry mentorships (aligns with Shih-Ting Lee's charge)
  • Alliance Project Internships: Create pathway for ACC students to contribute to Alliance-funded AI projects (paid internships)
  • Networking Event Priority: Secure student registration priority for Alliance networking events, workshops, and career fairs
  • Company Site Visits: Organize quarterly site visits to Alliance member companies (Austin AI startups, tech giants)

3
Scale Hackathon Model to Ongoing Programs

Scale AI Hackathon (2023) proved ACC can execute industry-partnered AI programs. Transform one-off event model into semester-long structured programs.

  • Semester-Long Cohorts: Instead of 1-day hackathon, create 16-week AI development cohorts with weekly industry mentorship
  • Tool Access Extension: Negotiate with Scale AI and other Alliance members for semester-long API access (not just event-day)
  • Industry Partner Rotation: Each semester features different Alliance member company (Scale AI → [Next Partner] → [Next Partner])
  • Portfolio Integration: Hackathon projects become portfolio pieces with industry review and feedback loops

4
Integrate with Committee Strategic Goals

Your proposal leverages infrastructure that committee members already approved (CGCS, Alliance membership). Position as implementation of existing institutional strategy.
