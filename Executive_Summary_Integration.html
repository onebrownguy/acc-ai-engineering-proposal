The 3 Charges - Integrated System

Charge 1: AI Tool Access Program

Problem: $1,920-$4,920/year tool costs lock ACC students out of AI careers

Solution: Tiered access program ($0-200/student/year)

  • Tier 1 (Free): Ollama, ChromaDB, GitHub Copilot - available to ALL students
  • Tier 2 ($50/student/year): Cloud credits, Pinecone, LangSmith - for course enrollments
  • Tier 3 ($200/student/year): OpenAI/Anthropic APIs, premium tools - for certification track
Year 1 Budget $13,500
Students Served 150 total
Cost Reduction 95%

Charge 2: Prompt Engineering & RAG Systems Curriculum

Problem: Zero ACC courses on prompt engineering, RAG systems, AI agents

Solution: 3-course certificate sequence (9 credits)

  • AIXX101 - Prompt Engineering Fundamentals (3 credits)
    Foundations, API integration, basic deployment
    3 portfolio projects (chatbot, code docs, Q&A system)
  • AIXX102 - RAG Systems & Vector Databases (3 credits)
    Vector databases, semantic search, RAG architecture
    3 portfolio projects (course catalog, legal/medical, custom domain)
  • AIXX103 - AI Agents & Advanced Orchestration (3 credits)
    Agent patterns, multi-agent systems, production engineering
    3 portfolio projects (research agent, code analysis, open capstone)
Year 1 Budget $93,200
Year 1 Revenue $133,500
ROI 43%

Charge 3: Portfolio Development & Mentorship Program

Problem: ACC students can't demonstrate AI skills (no portfolios, no industry connections)

Solution: 9-project portfolio + industry mentorship + ACC AI Engineer Certification

  • 9 portfolio projects across 3 courses (progressively complex)
  • 20+ industry mentors from Austin AI Alliance companies
  • Portfolio infrastructure (GitHub org, website, CI/CD)
  • Certification program with portfolio defense and industry evaluation
Budget Included in Charge 2
Job Placement Target 90%+
Starting Salary $100K+

Risk Assessment & Mitigation

Risk 1: Low Enrollment (What if only 15 students enroll?)

Likelihood: Medium
Impact: Medium

Impact: Revenue doesn't cover costs ($22,500 tuition vs. $95,200 budget)

Mitigation:

  • Aggressive marketing (start 6 months before launch)
  • Partner with local high schools (dual credit students)
  • Target working professionals (LinkedIn ads, bootcamp refugees)
  • Offer evening/weekend sections

Fallback: If enrollment too low Fall 2025, delay to Spring 2026 and use extra time for marketing

Risk 2: Can't Hire Qualified Instructor

Likelihood: Medium-High
Impact: High

Impact: No one to teach courses (program can't launch)

Mitigation:

  • Start hiring process February 2025 (6 months before Fall start)
  • Competitive salary ($84K = market rate)
  • Recruit from UT Austin AI lab alumni, local startups, bootcamp instructors
  • Backup: Adjunct team (3 part-time vs. 1 full-time)

Fallback: Partner with UT Austin for adjunct instructor

Risk 3: Industry Mentors Don't Commit

Likelihood: Low-Medium
Impact: Medium

Mitigation:

  • Recruit 30 mentors (need 20, 33% buffer for dropouts)
  • Low commitment (1-2 hours/month, flexible scheduling)
  • Offer company benefits (hiring pipeline, tax deduction)

Fallback: Launch with faculty-only mentorship, add industry later

Risk 4: Tool Costs Exceed Budget

Likelihood: Low
Impact: Medium

Mitigation:

  • Free tier strategy (Ollama, ChromaDB, GitHub Copilot = $0)
  • Negotiate educational discounts with vendors
  • Industry sponsorships cover tool costs ($5K/year)

Fallback: Fully free tier only (Ollama + ChromaDB)

Risk 5: Graduates Don't Get Jobs (< 50% placement)

Likelihood: Low
Impact: High

Mitigation:

  • Portfolio quality standards (ensure projects are impressive)
  • Industry mentor connections (warm introductions)
  • Job search support (resume reviews, interview prep)
  • Austin AI Alliance job board

Fallback: Pivot marketing from "guaranteed jobs" to "skills development"

Risk 6: AI Technology Changes Rapidly

Likelihood: High
Impact: Medium

Mitigation:

  • Modular curriculum design (update examples, not structure)
  • Focus on fundamentals (prompt engineering, RAG = stable)
  • Quarterly curriculum reviews (faculty + industry advisors)
  • Annual course refreshes

Fallback: Agile mid-semester updates if industry shifts
