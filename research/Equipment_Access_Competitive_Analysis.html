‚ö†Ô∏è ACC's Competitive Equipment Gaps

1. No Makerspace Fabrication Equipment

Gap: ACC lacks 3D printers, laser cutters, CNC machines, and fabrication tools that are standard at peer institutions (including community colleges).

Impact: Students in Design, Manufacturing, Construction & Applied Technologies (DMC&AT), Engineering, and Computer Science cannot prototype physical projects or learn industry-standard fabrication workflows.

2. No VR/AR Equipment Access

Gap: UT Austin, Texas State, and multiple community colleges provide VR/AR headsets for student checkout. ACC has none publicly available.

Impact: Students interested in game development, immersive media, healthcare simulation, or construction visualization cannot access emerging technology that competitors provide.

3. No High-Performance Computing (GPU Clusters)

Gap: UT Austin and Texas A&M provide remote HPC cluster access with GPUs for machine learning, AI training, and scientific computing. ACC has no equivalent.

Impact: Computer Science and Software Development students cannot train AI models, run large-scale simulations, or learn GPU programming without personal workstations (financial barrier).

4. Limited CAD Workstation Access

Gap: While ACC provides CAD software via OnTheHub, access to high-performance CAD workstations (like Texas A&M's makerspace design stations) is unclear/limited.

Impact: Engineering and design students may lack hardware capable of running complex CAD assemblies, simulations, or rendering tasks.
