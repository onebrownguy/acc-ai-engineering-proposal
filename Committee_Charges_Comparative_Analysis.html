Potential Conflicts & Resolutions

Conflict 1: Scope Creep from Governance Charges

Issue: Multiple members (Emilie, David, Keri, Manuel, Sean) proposing governance frameworks. Risk of paralysis-by-analysis delaying technical implementation.

Resolution: Position Abel's charges as "pilot initiative" that can be governed by existing frameworks. Don't wait for perfect governance - launch pilot (Course 1, Fall 2025) with basic acceptable use policy, refine governance as program scales.

Message: "We can govern my pilot within existing ACC IT policies. Perfect governance shouldn't delay student opportunity. Launch pilot, learn, adapt governance."

Conflict 2: General Literacy vs. Technical Depth

Issue: Many members want "AI literacy for all" (Marisa, Keri, Theresa, Jennifer). Risk of diluting Abel's technical curriculum into shallow survey course.

Resolution: Two-pathway model (already in Abel's original proposal): General pathway (literacy for all) + Technical pathway (Abel's 3 courses). Course 1 serves BOTH - accessible to all majors (gen ed competency) but also gateway to technical deepening.

Message: "Course 1 = general AI literacy. Courses 2-3 = technical specialization. We serve both audiences without dilution."

Conflict 3: Academic Integrity Concerns

Issue: Multiple members concerned about AI cheating (La Tasha, Chris, Toño, Jason, Pamela). May resist AI tool access fearing misuse.

Resolution: Abel's program TEACHES students to BUILD AI systems (not use AI to cheat). Unique AI use policy: "This is a course ABOUT AI, so AI use is required but must be cited, iterated, and understood."

Message: "My courses teach students to create AI tools, not cheat with them. Academic integrity concerns are orthogonal - they apply to students using ChatGPT on English essays, not students building RAG systems in CS courses."

Strategic Insights & Recommendations

What Was Detected in Committee Landscape

Insight 1: Abel Has the Only Technical Implementation

Of 28 submitted charges, ONLY Abel's provides detailed technical curriculum with specific courses, tools, and implementation plans. Everyone else focuses on governance, policy, literacy, or academic integrity.

Strategic Advantage: No competition. Other charges create the ENVIRONMENT (governance, policies, partnerships). Abel's charges provide the PROGRAM (courses, certification, student outcomes).

Insight 2: Strong Alignment on Workforce Preparation

7 members (Marisa, Ron, Theresa, Darin, Shih-Ting, Jennifer, + Suyeon) explicitly want AI training aligned with workforce needs. Abel's verified job market research (2,000+ postings from Indeed: 1,224, LinkedIn: 2,000+, Glassdoor: 1,349 - October 14, 2025) VALIDATES their goals with data.

Strategic Advantage: Abel provides the EVIDENCE others are seeking. His charges implement their vision.

Insight 3: Manuel Duran is Critical Swing Vote

As Assistant Dean for CSIT (Abel's division), Manuel oversees the programs where this would live. His charge envisions "positioning ACC as national AI leader" - Abel's proposal IS that positioning (first CC with RAG specialization).

Strategic Advantage: Court Manuel as primary champion. He has institutional authority to approve curriculum in his division.

Insight 4: No Charges Address RAG Specialization

Zero other members mention RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) systems specifically. This is Abel's unique contribution - and it's the dominant AI architecture (70%+ of production AI apps).

Strategic Advantage: Position as "filling the gap." Committee has general literacy covered. Abel provides specialized technical training that's missing nationwide in community colleges.

Insight 5: Governance Charges Enable (Not Block) Technical Program

Initial concern: Would governance charges slow down technical implementation? FINDING: No. Governance charges (Emilie, David, Keri, Sean) create the INFRASTRUCTURE Abel's program needs (tool vetting, acceptable use policies, data security).

Strategic Advantage: Don't position Abel's charges as competing with governance. Position as "the pilot program governance frameworks will be tested on."

Insight 6: Academic Integrity Concerns Are Red Herring

7 members focused on AI cheating detection. This applies to students using ChatGPT to write essays - NOT to CS students learning to build AI systems.

Strategic Response: Preemptively address this in Friday pitch: "My courses teach students to BUILD AI tools, not misuse them. We're training future AI engineers, not preventing ChatGPT misuse in English courses."
